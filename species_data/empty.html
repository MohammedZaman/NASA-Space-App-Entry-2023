Chlorophyll is vital for the existence of phytoplankton.

  • Phytoplankton serves as an indicator organism for the health of a water body.
  • Monitoring chlorophyll levels helps track algal growth.
  • Waters with high chlorophyll usually have high levels of nutrients, particularly phosphorus and nitrogen.
  • These nutrients trigger algal growth or bloom.
  • Algal blooms can deplete dissolved oxygen levels when they crash and die, often causing fish kills.
  • High levels of nitrogen and phosphorus may indicate pollution from man-made sources like septic system leakage, malfunctioning wastewater treatment plants, or fertilizer runoff.
  • Therefore, chlorophyll measurement can indirectly indicate nutrient levels.
